# v0.44.1

**Summary:** Harden vault_registry.py typed-local reads and non-local entries

- `src/brain-core/scripts/vault_registry.py` now writes typed `local` records as `<brain-id>\tlocal\t<absolute-vault-path>`, still reads legacy two-column local entries, preserves non-local rows opaquely, and raises `RegistryReadError` when the authoritative registry exists but cannot be read.
- `cli/brain`, `src/brain-core/scripts/_bootstrap/workspace_binding.py`, and `src/brain-core/scripts/_machine/discovery.py` now treat the user-home authoritative registry as the local-only routing source, consume the stable `value` locator field, and surface unreadable-registry failures honestly instead of falling back to stale machine state.
- `tests/test_vault_registry.py`, `tests/test_brain_cli.py`, `tests/test_init.py`, `tests/test_setup.py`, `tests/test_machine.py`, `tests/test_configure.py`, and `tests/test_install_script_vault_registry.py` cover typed-local round-trip, reserved and unknown non-local kinds, unreadable-registry failures, and non-local preservation across `unregister()` / `prune()`.
- `docs/architecture/decisions/dd-051-workspace-owned-binding-and-workspace-aware-mcp.md`, `docs/functional/scripts.md`, `docs/user/user-reference.md`, and `src/brain-core/scripts/README.md` now describe the shipped authoritative-registry contract as typed `local` entries today with reserved non-local kinds that remain unroutable until a future transport design lands.
